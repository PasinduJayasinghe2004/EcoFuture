Climate Change & Biodiversity

Why Understanding climate change Matter

Climate change is disrupting the natural world and endangering human health. From rising temperatures to disappearing species, its effects are deep, far-reaching, and urgent.

How Climate Change Affects Biodiversity and Health

Impact Area Description
Species Extinction Many animals and plants are losing their habitats due to warming temperatures and deforestation. Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction.
Human Health Risks Climate change increases the spread of diseases, heat-related illnesses, and respiratory problems caused by pollution and wildfires.
Coral Bleaching Warming oceans cause coral reefs to bleach and die, affecting marine biodiversity and communities that rely on them for food and income.
Food Security Extreme weather disrupts farming and fishing, threatening food supplies, especially in vulnerable regions.
Water Scarcity Changes in rainfall and melting glaciers affect freshwater availability, risking droughts and poor sanitation.

What can we do to Reduce climate Impact?

  1. Use Renewable Energy:Switch to wind,solar and other clean sources of power.
  2. Protect Forest:Forest absorb carbon dioxide and support wildlife
  3. Reduce Meat ConsumptionLiverstock farming contributes heavily to greenhouse gases.
  4. Drive Less:Walk,Cycle or use public transport to cut down emissions
  5. Support green policies:Vote for leaders and laws that address climate change.
